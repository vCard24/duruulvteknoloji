Door Materials Buying Guide

Pros and cons of wood, steel, aluminium, PVC and fibreglass doors.

Doors are more than objects that open and close; they are a connection point between two spaces. Choosing the right door for both appearance and function matters. Design, manufacturing, and the materials used determine a door's aesthetic and structural value.

In this article, we review the advantages and disadvantages of five common door materials to help you find the best fit for your home.

1. Wooden doors

Wood is one of the most classic and popular door materials. It brings warmth and a natural character to your home.

Advantages:

  • Wide variety of wood species and color options
  • Suits both modern and rustic interiors

Disadvantages:

  • Susceptible to rot and insects
  • Can swell and warp in humid conditions
  • Requires regular maintenance and refinishing
  • Relatively expensive

2. Steel doors

When security is the priority, steel doors lead the field. Made from reinforced steel, they are available in a range of specifications.

Advantages:

  • High security
  • Durable and resistant to forced entry
  • Sound and thermal insulation
  • Strong, prestigious appearance

Disadvantages:

  • Needs care with suitable cleaners to resist corrosion

3. Aluminum doors

Favored for a modern, industrial look.

Advantages:

  • Lightweight, durable, and long-lasting
  • Can be powder-coated in many colors
  • Not affected by moisture; won't swell or crack

Disadvantages:

  • May oxidize in coastal areas due to salt air

4. PVC doors

Manufactured from PVC profiles with galvanized steel reinforcement inside.

Advantages:

  • Light, durable, and rust-resistant
  • Security can be enhanced with multi-point locking
  • Easy to maintain; wipe with a damp cloth

Disadvantages:

  • Color may fade or yellow over time
  • Limited design and color options

5. Fiberglass (composite) doors

A modern door material offering high durability.

Advantages:

  • Water-resistant, non-porous, and durable
  • Resistant to scratches and impacts
  • Can mimic the look of wood
  • Low maintenance

Disadvantages:

  • Among the more expensive options on the market

Conclusion

Choosing a door is not only about aesthetics; it also concerns durability and security. Wood adds warmth, steel strengthens protection, aluminum delivers a contemporary feel, PVC offers practicality, and fiberglass/composite is a robust, long-life alternative.

Selecting the right material for your home and climate ensures comfort and safety for years to come.
